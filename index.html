01How this list was built

v1 of this brief was an honest smart-guess — 20 recognizable names filtered by category. v2 replaces judgment with a repeatable method. Three steps:

1 · Comprehensive screen

Pulled the complete verified exhibitor feed (1,382 companies, real booths) and screened every monetizable category — not just brands we recognized. That surfaced fits recognition missed: AirFleet Capital, SkyWatch, Wings Insurance, American Bonanza Society.

2 · Weighted rubric

Every candidate scored /100 on six GlobalAir-specific dimensions (below), weighted toward audience overlap and monetization fit. Same rubric, every account — so the ranking is comparable, not vibes.

3 · Company + individual audit

Each finalist researched as a company (marketing posture, gaps we fill, leverage) and as individuals (the real decision-maker, sourced & cited). Named contacts are labeled verified or role target — never invented.

What the rigor changed vs v1

  • FLYING Magazine → reclassified as a competitor. Its owner, Firecrown Media, owns AircraftForSale.com and AvBuyer — a direct marketplace rival. Moved to competitive intel, not targets.
  • Sporty's dropped out of the 20. The audit found it runs its own competing media network (Air Facts, iPad Pilot News) — a conflicted advertiser, so it scores 69 and sits below the line.
  • Four missed accounts scored in: AirFleet Capital, SkyWatch, Wings Insurance, American Bonanza Society.
  • Dropped from v1's 20: Sporty's, Van's, Piper, Shell, AssuredPartners — outscored on the rubric.

10Method & sourcing

  • Universe: full verified EAA/goeshow exhibitor feed (1,382 companies, real booths), retrieved 2026-07-01. Saved as OSH_2026_Exhibitor_List_SOURCE.tsv in this folder.
  • Screen: every monetizable category reviewed (FBO/airport, insurance, finance, avionics, flight-planning, OEM, engines/props, retail/gear, fuel, media/associations) — not just recognized brands.
  • Scoring: the six-dimension weighted rubric (§02) applied identically to 24 finalists.
  • Audit: each finalist researched as a company (marketing posture, gaps, leverage) and to a decision-maker, via web/LinkedIn/press.
  • Contacts: labeled Verified (sourced & cited in research notes) or role target (confirm pre-show). No names were invented. Where a person's status is in flux (AOPA leadership, Continental CEO through the Arcline close, Textron's departed Rob Scholl) it is flagged.
  • Re-verify before use: booth assignments can shift — re-pull the feed the week before; confirm named contacts on LinkedIn at outreach; dates shown are the published EAA AirVenture 2026 window (Jul 20–26).
